DeFX: Guitar Effect Removal

Audio examples — companion page for the DeFX paper

DeFX removes distortion, reverb, delay, and other effects from guitar recordings using a fine-tuned Hybrid Demucs backbone. Listen to the examples below to compare the wet (effected) input, the DeFX restored output, and the dry (clean) ground truth.

Use headphones for the best comparison. All audio is 44.1 kHz mono.

Examples

Amp Distortion Only

Blackpanel tube amp at moderate gain — the core use case for DeFX.

Amp + Room Reverb

Amp distortion with a medium room reverb. DeFX reduces both the distortion and the reverb tail.

Amp + Delay + Reverb

A more complex chain with slapback delay and hall reverb on top of amp distortion.

Chorus + Reverb (No Amp)

Clean guitar with chorus modulation and reverb — chorus is particularly challenging because its pitch fluctuations are difficult to distinguish from the natural pitch variations of a guitar.

About

DeFX uses a pretrained HDemucs source separation model with selectively unfrozen encoder/decoder layers and a learned mixing head. Training data is generated synthetically using Neural Amp Modeler captures and Spotify Pedalboard effects.
